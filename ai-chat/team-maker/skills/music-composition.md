# Music Composition & Mixing

## Composition

### Song Structure
- **Intro** — establish mood, 4-8 bars
- **Verse** — narrative, lower energy, build tension
- **Pre-chorus** — transitional lift, rising melody or harmonic shift
- **Chorus** — hook, highest energy, most memorable melody
- **Bridge** — contrast section, new chord progression or rhythm, appears once
- **Outro** — resolve or fade, callback to intro motif

### Melody
- Stepwise motion for verses (small intervals, singable)
- Leaps for choruses (fourths, fifths, octaves — emotional impact)
- Repetition with variation: repeat a phrase, change the ending
- Rests are melodic — silence creates anticipation

### Harmony
- I-IV-V-I for resolution and stability
- vi-IV-I-V for emotional pop progressions
- Modal interchange: borrow chords from parallel minor for color (bVII, iv, bVI)
- Pedal tones under changing chords for tension
- Voice leading: move each note the shortest distance to the next chord

### Rhythm
- Syncopation adds energy — accent the "and" beats
- Swing vs straight: swing for groove, straight for drive
- Polyrhythm: 3-over-2 or 4-over-3 for complexity
- Half-time feel in a bridge to create contrast
- Silence on beat 1 occasionally — makes re-entry powerful

## Arrangement

### Layering
- Start sparse, add elements each section
- Each instrument owns a frequency range — don't stack everything in the mids
- Double vocals or leads an octave apart for width
- Counter-melodies in verses keep interest without competing with the vocal
- Remove elements to create dynamics — a dropout before the chorus hits harder than adding more

### Instrumentation by Genre
- **Electronic**: synth pads (atmosphere), bass synth (low end), drums (programmed), arps (movement), vocals/topline
- **Rock**: guitar (rhythm + lead), bass, drums, keys optional, vocals
- **Hip-hop**: 808s (sub bass), sampled drums, melodic loops, vocal chops, lead vocal
- **Orchestral**: strings (sustain), brass (power), woodwinds (color), percussion (punctuation), harp/piano (detail)

## Mixing

### Signal Chain
1. **Gain staging** — peaks around -18 to -12 dBFS per track, master bus peaking at -6
2. **EQ** — cut before boost, high-pass everything that doesn't need sub frequencies
3. **Compression** — tame dynamics, 3-6 dB gain reduction for most tracks
4. **Spatial** — panning, reverb, delay
5. **Bus processing** — glue compressor on groups, subtle saturation for warmth

### Frequency Ranges
| Range | Hz | Instruments |
|-------|-----|-------------|
| Sub | 20-60 | Kick, 808, bass synth |
| Bass | 60-250 | Bass guitar, low synths, floor tom |
| Low-mid | 250-500 | Guitar body, piano warmth, vocal fullness |
| Mid | 500-2k | Vocal presence, snare body, guitar attack |
| Upper-mid | 2k-5k | Vocal clarity, hi-hat attack, guitar bite |
| Presence | 5k-10k | Vocal air, cymbal shimmer, string detail |
| Air | 10k-20k | Sparkle, breath, room ambience |

### EQ Guidelines
- High-pass vocals at 80-100 Hz
- Cut 200-400 Hz mudiness on guitars and keys
- Boost 3-5 kHz on vocals for presence (narrow Q)
- Cut competing frequencies rather than boosting the one you want to hear
- Reference against a commercial mix in the same genre

### Compression
- Vocals: fast attack (5-15ms), medium release, 4:1 ratio
- Drums: medium attack (let transient through), fast release, 4:1
- Bass: fast attack, slow release, 4:1-8:1
- Bus: slow attack (30ms+), auto release, 2:1, 1-3 dB reduction

### Spatial
- **Panning**: drums centered (kick, snare), overheads and hats wide, bass centered, guitars L/R, vocals center
- **Reverb**: short plate for vocals, room for drums, hall for pads — pre-delay 20-40ms to keep source upfront
- **Delay**: stereo ping-pong for width, tempo-synced for rhythmic elements, slapback for rockabilly/vocal doubling
- Less reverb = more intimate, more reverb = more epic

### Loudness
- Mix with monitors at a consistent, moderate level
- Check on headphones and phone speakers
- Master bus limiter: target -14 LUFS for streaming, -9 LUFS for club
- Leave headroom for mastering — don't slam the limiter yourself
